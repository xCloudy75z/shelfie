Shelfie

A deliberately simple UAE grocery price + budget tracker. One person, one PIN, three tabs.

The one-line pitch: Standing at the shelf — is this price actually good, or have I paid less before? Log purchases (or import the Carrefour PDF), know each item's price story, and keep an eye on the month. Nothing else.

1 · Who & why

A personal tool for one owner (you), used in three real moments:

🛒 At the shelf
Is this price good, or have I paid less? → the Prices tab.
🧾 After shopping
Log what I bought — fast. → the Log tab + PDF import.
📅 End of month
Over or under budget, where did it go? → the Month tab.

2 · The whole app is 3 tabs

Tab 1 — Log

Fast logging is the headline. Two ways in:

  • Import receipt (PDF) — upload the Carrefour PDF they email you. A free, on-device parser extracts every item + price, you review the list (fix any oddity), and save the whole trip in one tap. Turns a 30-item monthly haul from 30 taps into one upload + a glance.
  • Quick manual entry — for a single forgotten item: Item (autocompletes from your history), Price, Qty (default 1), Store (defaults Carrefour), Category (auto-fills for known items), Date (today), optional "on offer" toggle.

Tab 2 — Prices the payoff

Search an item and see its price story: Last paid · Best ever · Average · Highest (with dates), a small trend line, and recent buys. Plus the shelf check: type the price in front of you and get a plain verdict — Great price / Cheaper than usual / About the same / Pricier than usual.

Tab 3 — Month

Spend this month vs. your budget with a green/amber/red pace, a category breakdown ("where it went"), recent purchases, and a button to set the monthly budget. Flip back to past months.

🔒 PIN lock A single 4-digit PIN opens the app, remembered on your device so you rarely re-type it.

3 · The receipt parser (the one clever bit)

Your Carrefour PDFs are text-based (confirmed from a real sample), so we read them with a free, deterministic parser — no AI, no per-scan cost, no external service. Each line gives us item description, quantity, unit price, and line total, and the receipt's grand total lets the app self-check the parse ("total matches ✓").

Because layouts can vary, the parser gets hardened against real samples ("try to break it") before it ships. If a future receipt ever fails to parse cleanly, you fall back to quick manual entry — nothing breaks.

4 · Under the hood

PieceChoice
FoundationStandalone personal web app on Vercel — no portal SSO, no multi-user plumbing.
StackNext.js + TypeScript, server actions (no separate API layer), Tailwind.
DatabasePostgres on Neon. ~5 tables: Item, Category, Purchase, Budget, Settings.
Price historyCalculated live from your purchases — no caching machinery.
LoginOne 4-digit PIN (stored hashed) + a remembered session. No accounts system.
MobileLight PWA touch — installs to your home screen, loads instantly.
MoneyStored as whole fils (no rounding drift), shown as AED.

5 · What we're deliberately NOT building

The current FilsWise carries all of this. Shelfie drops every bit of it:

Portal SSO / multi-user Offer-authenticity engine Trips + merge/split Tags system Budget Coach Investment insights Timing "best day" insights Ramadan engine Alerts + real-time SSE Web-push Barcode scanning Watchlists Excel export

From 20 data tables → ~5. From 37 API routes → none (server actions). From ~16 services → a handful.
