Shelfie — Spec

A deliberately simple UAE grocery price + budget tracker. One person, one PIN, three tabs.

The pitch: Standing at the shelf — is this price actually good, or have I paid less before? Log purchases (or import the Carrefour PDF), know each item's price story, watch the month. Nothing else.

2 · The three tabs

Tab 1 — Log

Fast logging is the headline. Two ways in:

  • Import receipt (PDF) — upload the Carrefour PDF they email you. A free, on-device parser reads every line, then shows you a review screen of draft rows — never a silent insert. You glance, fix, bulk-set categories, and save the whole trip in one tap.
  • Quick manual entry — a single forgotten item: Item (autocompletes & matches your history), Price, Qty (default 1), Store (defaults Carrefour), Category (auto for known items), Date (today), optional "on offer".

Tab 2 — Prices the payoff

Search an item and see its price story — Last paid · Best ever · Average · Highest (with dates and store), a trend line, and recent buys. Then the shelf check: type the price in front of you and get a plain verdict — Great price / Cheaper than usual / About the same / Pricier than usual.

Tab 3 — Month

Spend this month vs. your budget with a green/amber/red pace, a category breakdown ("where it went"), recent purchases, and a set-budget button. Flip to past months.

🔒 PIN lock A short PIN opens the app, remembered on your device — with brute-force protection (below).

3 · Hardened by the review

The adversarial review found real correctness holes before any code. These fixes are now part of the design — and they keep the app simple, just correct:

Unit price, always. All price-history math compares total ÷ quantity, never the raw line total — so a 6-pack line never distorts a single-unit verdict. Weighed produce compares per-kg to per-kg.
One item, one identity. Manual and imported names run through a normalized fuzzy match with an "is this the same item?" confirm before a new item is created — plus a merge tool to fix any fragments. No silent junk items.
Import is draft-first. Parsed lines are self-checked against the receipt's grand total (±0.05). On any mismatch you land in an editable review screen — nothing saves until you confirm. Re-importing the same receipt is caught by a fingerprint and warns you.
Honest verdicts. Benchmarks use non-offer prices over a rolling window (so a one-off promo or a 2-year-old price can't poison "best ever"), and the verdict is suppressed until there are enough samples to judge.
Real security. PIN attempts are throttled server-side (lockout after repeated fails), sessions are httpOnly with a sane expiry + a manual "Lock", and your data is one tap to export (JSON/CSV) — never trapped, never a single point of loss.
Right month, every time. Purchases bucket by date in Asia/Dubai, so a late-night buy on the 31st never slips into the wrong month's budget.

4 · The receipt parser

Your Carrefour PDFs are text-based (confirmed from a real sample), so a free deterministic parser reads them — no AI, no per-scan cost, no external service. Each line yields description, quantity, unit price, and line total; the grand total self-checks the parse.

Layouts vary (VAT columns, bag fees, discount lines, weighed items, multi-page), so the parser is hardened against real receipts ("try to break it") and always routes through the review screen. If a receipt ever won't parse cleanly, you fall back to quick manual entry — nothing breaks.

5 · Under the hood

PieceChoice
FoundationStandalone personal web app on Vercel — no portal SSO, no multi-user plumbing.
StackNext.js + TypeScript, server actions (no separate API layer), Tailwind.
DatabasePostgres on Neon. ~6 tables: Item, Category, Purchase, Budget, Settings, ReceiptImport (fingerprint + undo).
Price historyComputed live from purchases (unit price) — no cached stats table.
LoginPIN (hashed) + throttle + httpOnly session + Lock. No accounts system.
OfflineLight PWA; recently-viewed item stats cached client-side so the shelf check works on weak signal.
MoneyWhole fils (no rounding drift), shown as AED. Dubai-time date buckets.
Your dataOne-tap export (JSON/CSV). It's yours, always retrievable.
