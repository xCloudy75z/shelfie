Receipt Import v2

Barcodes as the true identity of a product — so receipt names and your own names finally line up. Plus per-item “on offer”, and a built-in accuracy check.

The idea in one line: every real product line on the receipt is followed by its barcode. We start using that barcode as the product's real identity — so “A.G PCORN EBTR273G” and your “Americana Garden popcorn” become the same item, automatically, from now on.

What we're fixing

1. Cryptic names split your history. Today items match by name only, so a receipt's short code never lines up with what you type by hand → two items → wrong price story.

2. No “on offer” on import. Every imported item is treated as full price, so a promo can wrongly become your “best price ever”.

3. “Is the item count right?” You saw 30 items and weren't sure. There was no self-check.

How the barcode fix works

Reading receipts

1
Each product line's barcode is captured and stored with the item (hidden — you never have to look at it).
2
Next time any receipt has that barcode, it's filed under your friendly name automatically — no cryptic duplicate, ever again.
3
First time a barcode is new, it comes in with the receipt name; on the review screen you can rename it and/or tap “same as my [item]”. Whatever you choose is remembered against that barcode forever. You're never forced — you can just save and tidy later.

Manual entry (the two-way bridge)

A new optional “Barcode” field on the manual form. When you enter one:

  • Already known → links automatically, fills in your friendly name.
  • New, but the name matches something you have → attaches the barcode to that item (so future receipts of it auto-match).
  • Genuinely new → saves as a new item with that barcode.

Skip the field and everything works exactly as today.

The accuracy self-check

A genuine product always has a barcode right under it; a TOTAL / DISCOUNT / heading line does not. So any row we pick up with no barcode gets a small “check this” flag on the review screen for you to confirm or remove. Nothing junk slips in — and nothing real is ever dropped.

On offer

A per-item “on offer” checkbox on the review screen (just like manual entry). Ticked items are correctly kept out of your “best price” comparisons.

Built to not break later

You asked for a genuine build, no fixing afterwards. So before any code, the design gets an adversarial review — I try hard to break it. Cases it must survive:

  • Same barcode twice on one receipt (bought 2) → one item, two clean lines.
  • Barcode matches your item but the receipt name differs → barcode wins.
  • A real item with no barcode (weighed produce, carrier bag) → flagged, never dropped.
  • The receipt's bottom transaction barcode → never mistaken for a product.
  • Re-importing the same receipt after you renamed items → still caught as a duplicate.
  • A typed barcode that clashes with a different product → no silent overwrite.
Finish gate: not “done” until it's proven on the real Vercel app AND your iPhone with a real receipt — not just local tests. That's the exact gap that let the earlier import bug through.
